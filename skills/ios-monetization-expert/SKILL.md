## Common Indie Dev Scenarios

### "Should I add a weekly plan?"
**Yes, if** you're in Productivity, Utilities, or Photo & Video. Weekly drives the majority of revenue in those categories and LTV is competitive ($46+ in Productivity/Utilities). **Critical:** pair it with a free trial — day-30 retention jumps from 23% → 42%.

### "Are my numbers good?"
Ask: app category, plan type, install-to-paid rate, day-30 retention, price point, primary market. Then benchmark directly against the tables above.

### "Should I raise my prices?"
Almost certainly yes. High-priced apps earn 3x the LTV. For weekly plans, higher prices actually improve conversion. Use the pricing index — if you charge the same in Germany as in Turkey, you're undercharging Germany by 4.4x.

### "Should I use a free trial?"
**Depends on category and plan:**
- Weekly/monthly in Utilities, Health & Fitness, Education → YES, strong LTV uplift
- Productivity, Lifestyle, Entertainment, Graphics & Design → test direct paywall, may outperform
- Trial users retain 8–60% better at first renewal

### "My churn is high"
- Weekly: 65% cancel in month 1 — this is normal. Track LTV, not month-1 retention.
- Monthly: 43% at day 90 is benchmark — below that, fix value delivery in weeks 2–4
- Annual: spike at renewal is expected — add a win-back flow 30 days before renewal

### "I should expand globally"
Prioritize high-LTV markets you may be ignoring: Switzerland ($28.5), Qatar ($27.5), Israel ($27.0), UK ($23.6), Japan ($23.4) — all above or near the US ($19.9). Don't use flat global pricing.

### "My refund rate is over 5%"
Above 4.2% on annual is a warning sign. Audit paywall copy for overpromising and check whether users hit the core value before the refund window closes.

## Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

**Source:** https://adapty.io/blog/customer-acquisition-cost/

### CAC Formula
CAC = Total Marketing & Sales Spend / Number of New Customers Acquired

### Mobile App CAC Benchmarks
| Target Action | iOS | Android |
|--------------|-----|---------|
| Install | $2–$3 | $1–$2 |
| Registration | $7.55 | $2.17 |
| In-app purchase | $77.45 | $86.72 |

### LTV:CAC Ratio
- **Ideal ratio: 3:1 or higher** — you earn $3 for every $1 spent acquiring a user
- Quick rule: take your 12-month LTV and divide by 2 — that's your maximum sustainable CAC
- Below 3:1 → you're overspending on acquisition or undermonetizing
- Above 5:1 → you may be underinvesting in growth

### CAC Optimization Tips for Indie Devs
- Focus on organic channels (ASO, content, referrals) to keep CAC low
- Track CAC per channel — not just blended CAC
- A high CAC is fine IF your LTV supports it; always think in ratios
- Reducing churn is often cheaper than reducing CAC

## ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)

**Source:** https://adapty.io/blog/average-revenue-per-unit-arpu/

### ARPU Formula
ARPU = Total Revenue in Period / Total Users in Same Period

Example: $5,000 from 100 annual subscriptions → ARPU = $50 per annual sub

### ARPU Benchmarks
- Mobile gaming app market average ARPU: **~$57.64** (rising to ~$64.26 in 2027)
- General mobile apps baseline: **~$0.04 per user** (includes free users)
- Subscriptions generate **4.6x higher ARPU** compared to ad-only apps

### How to Increase ARPU
- Use ARPU to identify your most revenue-generating product/plan
- Compare ARPU across plan types (weekly vs monthly vs annual) to find winners
- Segment ARPU by country — high-LTV markets deserve more attention
- Upsell existing users to higher tiers rather than only chasing new installs
- Track ARPU trends over time — declining ARPU signals pricing or retention problems

## LTV Calculation Deep Dive

**Source:** https://adapty.io/blog/customer-lifetime-value-ltv/

### LTV Formulas

**Simple formula:**
LTV = ARPU × Average Customer Lifetime (in months)

**Subscription formula:**
LTV = Average Monthly Revenue per Customer / Monthly Churn Rate

**SaaS/advanced formula:**
LTV = [(Base ARPU + Expansion Revenue) × Gross Margin] × Customer Lifetime

### Key LTV Principles
- Always calculate **realized LTV** (net of refunds), not gross LTV
- iOS refund window is up to 14 days — factor this into short-term LTV calculations
- LTV should be calculated per cohort (month of acquisition), not as a single blended number
- Market conditions change — recalculate LTV benchmarks at least quarterly
- Compare LTV across acquisition channels to find your most valuable users
- **LTV is the single most important metric** for deciding how much to spend on acquisition

## App Pricing Models (Top 5 Strategies)

**Source:** https://adapty.io/blog/app-pricing-models/

### 1. Weekly Plans as Core Strategy
- Weekly subscriptions capture **47% of total revenue** across subscription apps
- Low commitment beats low prices — users prefer trying cheap before committing big
- Pair with free trials for maximum conversion

### 2. Free Trials for Conversion
- Trials reduce commitment anxiety and filter for highest-value customers
- US sees **64% LTV increase** with trials; Europe sees **58% LTV increase**
- Trials serve dual purpose: convert users AND qualify leads

### 3. Premium Pricing
- Higher prices attract users who value your solution enough to commit
- Improves both revenue AND retention (premium users churn less)
- High-priced apps earn **3x the LTV** of low-priced apps

### 4. Tiered Pricing
- Tiered pricing increases revenue by **25–40%** vs single-tier models
- Offer clear differentiation between tiers — avoid a "mushy middle"
- Good/Better/Best structure works for most app categories

### 5. Localized Pricing
- Willingness to pay varies **2–3x between countries**
- Localized pricing is no longer optional — it's essential for maximizing revenue
- Use the pricing index (US = 1.0) to set country-specific prices

### Key Insight
Apps running continuous pricing experiments make **74% more MRR** than apps that don't test.

## Paywall Experiments Playbook

**Source:** https://adapty.io/blog/paywall-experiments-playbook/

### Testing Priority (What to Test First)
1. **Pricing first** — pricing experiments deliver **2–3x more uplift** than visual changes
2. **Offer structure** (trial length, plan combinations) second
3. **Visual/copy changes** third — important but lower leverage

Most teams test backwards (visuals first, pricing last). Don't make this mistake.

### Paywall A/B Test Ideas

**Pricing tests:**
- Test 20–30% price increases on weekly plans (higher prices often convert better)
- Test different trial lengths (3-day vs 7-day vs no trial)
- Test annual discount framing (save 75% vs $X/month vs total annual price)

**Structural tests:**
- Hard paywall (no close button) vs soft paywall (with close button)
- Hard paywalls work better for fitness apps
- Soft paywalls (subtle close option) work better for productivity & education
- Counterintuitively, adding a close button sometimes increases conversion

**Visual tests:**
- Video backgrounds drive **8–15% conversion increases** (must be high quality)
- Add star ratings / user testimonials (works best if you have strong reviews)
- Test number of plans shown (2 vs 3 options)

### Experiment Discipline
- **Winner = highest ARPU**, not highest conversion rate (a cheaper plan may convert better but earn less)
- Wait for **statistical significance** before declaring a winner
- Always have a test running — when one concludes, launch the next within a week
- Fast-growth apps treat experiments as a continuous process, not a one-off

## Mobile App Monetization Strategies

**Source:** https://adapty.io/blog/mobile-app-monetization-strategies/

### Market Reality
- **97% of Google Play apps** are free; **94.2% of iOS apps** are free
- Revenue comes from in-app monetization, not upfront purchase price

### Strategy Comparison
| Strategy | Best For | ARPU Impact |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Subscriptions | Utility, productivity, fitness | **4.6x higher ARPU** vs ads-only |
| In-app purchases (consumables) | Gaming | High per-user but volatile |
| Advertising | High-volume free apps | Low per-user, needs scale |
| Hybrid (subs + consumables) | Most top-grossing apps | Highest total revenue |

### Hybrid Monetization
- **35% of apps now mix subscriptions with consumables or lifetime purchases**
- Hybrid is the new standard for top-grossing apps
- Example: core features via subscription + one-time premium add-ons

### Hard Paywall vs Freemium
| Metric | Hard Paywall | Freemium |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Trial starts in week 1 | **78%** | 45% |
| Monthly retention | **12.8%** | 9.3% |

Hard paywalls force a decision, which filters for committed users. Freemium delays the decision, which can leak value.

### Web2App Strategy
- Acquire users through web channels, monetize via web-based payments
- Bypasses App Store commission entirely
- Some iOS developers report **65–120% revenue increases** after adding direct web checkout
- Best for apps with strong web presence or content marketing

### Reverse Trials
- Give users full premium access for a limited time, then downgrade to free
- Users experience the full value before deciding — reduces "what am I paying for?" objections
- Emerging strategy gaining traction in 2026

## Tiered Pricing Strategies

**Source:** https://adapty.io/blog/tiered-pricing/

### Revenue Impact
- Tiered pricing increases revenue by **25–40%** compared to single-tier models
- The "middle tier" is where most users land (decoy effect) — price it as your target

### The Hollow Middle Problem
- **41% of consumers** report subscription fatigue
- The $5–$10/month tier is declining — users are cutting generic utility apps
- Users want either a **clear deal** (cheap/free tier) or a **clear premium** (high value)
- Mid-priced plans without clear differentiation underperform both extremes

### Tier Design Principles
1. **Free tier:** Hook users, demonstrate core value, create habit
2. **Mid tier:** Most popular — include everything most users need (this is your anchor)
3. **Premium tier:** Power users, teams, or professionals — price 2.5–4x the mid tier

### Pricing Direction in 2026
- Successful apps moving toward **$30+/month** for apps delivering measurable outcomes
- High-volume ad-supported models for casual use cases
- Weekly plans work well for emerging markets and lower-intent buyers
- Localized pricing across tiers is essential — willingness to pay varies 2–3x by country

### What Works
- Clear feature gates between tiers (not just usage limits)
- Highlight the "most popular" tier visually on your paywall
- Use annual plans at a visible discount to anchor value perception
- Test 3 tiers vs 2 tiers — 3 often wins via the decoy effect

## Additional Indie Dev Scenarios

### "What's my CAC too high?"
Calculate your LTV:CAC ratio. Below 3:1 means you're overpaying for users. Fix by either increasing LTV (pricing, retention, upsells) or lowering CAC (better ASO, organic channels, improved ad targeting). Your 12-month LTV divided by 2 = your max sustainable CAC.

### "Should I try hybrid monetization?"
If you're subscription-only and leaving money on the table: yes. 35% of top apps now mix subs with consumables or lifetime purchases. Add one-time premium add-ons (templates, themes, export formats) alongside your subscription.

### "Should I use a hard or soft paywall?"
Hard paywalls: 78% trial starts in week 1 vs 45% for freemium. Better for fitness, photo/video, and utility apps. Soft paywalls: better for productivity and education where users need to experience value first. A/B test this — it's high-leverage.

### "Should I add a web checkout?"
If you have a web presence, yes. Some devs report 65–120% revenue increases by bypassing the 30% App Store cut. Works especially well for apps with strong content marketing or search traffic.

### "How should I structure my tiers?"
Use 3 tiers. Free (hook + habit), Mid (anchor, "most popular", core value), Premium (2.5–4x mid, power users). Avoid a mushy middle — each tier must have a clear reason to exist. Tiered pricing alone can lift revenue 25–40%.

### "Should I keep experimenting with my paywall?"
Always. Apps running continuous experiments make 74% more MRR. Test pricing first (2–3x more uplift than visual changes), then offers, then visuals. Always measure by ARPU, not conversion rate.
