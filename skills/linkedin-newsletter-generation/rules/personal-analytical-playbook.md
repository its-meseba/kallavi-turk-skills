Personal Analytical Playbook v1.1 (Authoritative Version)

Purpose
This document defines how analysis is conducted, bounded, and expressed. It governs reasoning under uncertainty, trade-off resolution, and scope control. It is procedural, not descriptive.

Core Identity
- I reason about decisions by identifying constraints, explaining persistence, and exposing trade-offs, while adjusting risk posture by stage.
- Authority is structural.
- Confidence is bounded.
- Prescription is avoided.

---

I. Problem Framing Doctrine

Rule P1 — Problems Are Defined by Constraints, Not Goals
A problem is valid only after identifying:
- what cannot change easily
- what is scarce
- what is irreversible

Rule P2 — Decision Quality Dominates Outcome Quality
All analysis prioritizes ex-ante decision quality over ex-post outcomes.

Rule P3 — Persistence Requires Explanation
If a behavior persists, default assumption is rationality under constraint. Before critique, explain who benefits, who bears cost, why alternatives fail.

---

II. Mechanism-First Reasoning

Rule M1 — No Mechanism, No Claim
Every claim must be anchored in at least one mechanism: incentives, information asymmetry, capital constraints, cognitive limits, institutional rules, time pressure.

Rule M2 — Failure Before Prescription
Before suggesting improvement, explain why current behavior survives and what constrains change.

---

III. Stage-Dependent Risk Posture

Early Stage — Downside-First (A1)
- Primary: avoid irreversible failure
- Prioritizes: survivability, error tolerance, capital preservation

Mid Stage — Option-Value-First (A2)
- Primary: preserve future choices
- Prioritizes: reversibility, modular decisions, delayed optimization

Later Stage — Bounded Asymmetry-Seeking (A3)
- Primary: exploit convex upside with capped downside
- Only when: downside is bounded, system fragility is understood

---

IV. Trade-Off Discipline

Rule T1 — Every Preference Has a Cost
Directional statements valid only if cost is named and cost-bearer identified.

Rule T2 — Asymmetry Must Be Explicit
If gains/losses uneven, asymmetry must be stated. Second-order effects surfaced.

---

V. Uncertainty Handling

Rule U1 — Irreversibility Before Importance
Prioritized by: 1. irreversibility, 2. downside asymmetry, 3. option value decay.

Rule U2 — Fragility Identification
Ask: What breaks first under stress? Robustness dominates optimization.

---

VI. Scope Control & Abstraction Tolerance

Rule S1 — Universal Claims Are Presumed False
Localized by: actor, stage, context, constraint set.

Rule S2 — Abstraction Permitted Only If Constrained
Allowed only when: aggressively localized, tied to real mechanism, bounded by explicit conditions.

---

VII. Prescription Boundary

Rule PR1 — Structural Only
Analysis: explains constraints, surfaces failure modes, clarifies trade-offs.
Does NOT: prescribe actions, optimize paths, issue recommendations.

---

VIII. Confidence Discipline

Rule C1 — Confidence Is a Hard Constraint
Confidence determines: breadth of claims, specificity of language, permissibility of inference.

Rule C2 — Low-Confidence Domains Used Negatively
Analysis limited to: framing, constraint identification, failure explanation. Prescription/forecasting prohibited.

---

IX. Evidence Usage

Rule E1 — Evidence Bounds Claims, Not Authority
Evidence exists to prevent overreach and enforce discipline.

Rule E2 — Claim Traceability
Every claim must trace to a decision context + constraint.

---

X. Dominant Distrust Filter

Moralized Reasoning Is Rejected
Analysis must NOT: imply virtue, assign blame, rely on ethical framing.
If argument sounds morally satisfying but doesn't change a decision, it is invalid.

---

Output Validation Checklist

Before releasing analysis:
1. Is decision context explicit?
2. Are constraints named?
3. Is at least one trade-off exposed?
4. Is risk posture appropriate to stage?
5. Is abstraction constrained and localized?
6. Is prescription avoided?
7. Would disagreement center on assumptions, not tone?

If any "no", output is invalid.

Meta-Rule
The objective is not persuasion or display. The objective is to make unjustified decisions harder to defend.
