Do Not Write About — Structural Exclusions v1.0 (Negative Scope Definition)

Purpose
This document defines what is excluded from public writing by structure, not by subject matter.
The goal is not censorship. The goal is signal protection: preventing dilution, drift, and unintended positioning.
Anything excluded here is excluded because it violates analytical integrity, not because it is uninteresting.

Global Exclusion Rule
If a piece cannot be written without relying on authority, motivation, or aspiration, it is excluded.
This rule supersedes all others.

Exclusion Class I — Authority-Dependent Content
Definition: Any content whose persuasive force depends on:
- personal experience
- proximity to events
- credentials
- outcomes achieved

Why It's Excluded
Authority is biographical, not analytical. This system requires authority to emerge from structure and reasoning alone.

Examples of Structural Violations
- "From my experience..."
- "What I've learned from..."
- "Having worked on X..."

Even if true, such framing shifts credibility away from reasoning.

Exclusion Class II — Motivational or Aspirational Framing
Definition: Any content that:
- Encourages
- Inspires
- Reassures
- Signals ambition or intent

Why It's Excluded
Motivation does not change decision quality. Aspirational framing replaces constraint analysis with sentiment.
Structural Marker: If removing emotional language collapses the argument, the content is excluded.

Exclusion Class III — Advice Without Constraint Ownership
Definition: Any recommendation that:
- Does not specify who bears the cost
- Does not name what is sacrificed
- Assumes compliance or execution

Why It's Excluded
Advice without cost attribution is moral preference disguised as analysis.
Structural Marker: If the reader can ask "who pays for this?" and the text cannot answer, it is excluded.

Exclusion Class IV — Universal Claims Without Localization
Definition: Statements that appear broadly applicable without being bounded by:
- context
- stage
- actor
- constraint set

Why It's Excluded
Universality is the fastest path to generic insight.
Structural Marker: If a claim still sounds valid after removing all context, it is excluded.

Exclusion Class V — Narrative Substitution for Mechanism
Definition: Content that explains outcomes using:
- chronology
- anecdotes
- storytelling
instead of incentives, frictions, or constraints.

Why It's Excluded
Narratives explain what happened, not why it persisted.
Structural Marker: If the explanation relies on sequence ("then", "after", "eventually") rather than causality, it is excluded.

Exclusion Class VI — Opinion Without Trade-Off Exposure
Definition: Directional statements ("better", "worse", "should") that:
- do not expose trade-offs
- do not acknowledge downside

Why It's Excluded
Preferences without trade-offs are opinions, not analysis.
Structural Marker: If the downside is implicit or omitted, the content is excluded.

Exclusion Class VII — Prediction-Centric Content
Definition: Any content whose value depends on:
- forecasting
- calling outcomes
- being right later

Why It's Excluded
This system evaluates decision quality ex ante, not correctness ex post.
Structural Marker: If the text ages poorly because the outcome changes, it is excluded.

Exclusion Class VIII — Tool, Tactic, or Framework Fetishism
Definition: Content focused on:
- tools
- templates
- named frameworks
without explaining why they work under specific constraints.

Why It's Excluded
Frameworks are compression devices, not substitutes for thinking.
Structural Marker: If the framework is the insight rather than the vehicle, the content is excluded.

Exclusion Class IX — Engagement-Oriented Writing
Definition: Content designed to:
- provoke discussion
- invite opinions
- solicit validation

Why It's Excluded
Engagement incentives distort reasoning density.
Structural Marker: If the ending asks a question or invites response, the content is excluded.

Exclusion Class X — Topics That Require Over-Claiming Given Current Confidence
Definition: Domains where:
- technical depth is required
- confidence is explicitly low

Why It's Excluded
Low confidence is a constraint. Writing beyond it forces over-claiming or vagueness.

Allowed Exception: You may write only to:
- explain failure modes
- surface constraints
- narrow claims

Never to:
- prescribe
- forecast
- optimize

Meta-Rule (Non-Negotiable)
If a piece could be read as signaling intelligence rather than clarifying a decision, it is excluded.
