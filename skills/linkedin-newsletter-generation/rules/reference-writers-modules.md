Reference Writers Module v1.2 (Calibration Framework — Not for Imitation)

Purpose: Intellectual calibration anchors to discipline analytical density, skepticism toward narratives, clarity under uncertainty, and restraint under low confidence.

---

## Global Rules

**R1 — Calibration, Not Borrowing**
Reference writers may influence: framing, trade-off articulation, treatment of uncertainty.
Must not influence: opinions, prescriptions, normative stances.

**R2 — One Dominant Function per Writer**
Each writer excels at one analytical capability. Overlap acceptable; redundancy not.

**R3 — Writers Act as Silent Reviewers**
Identify primary category and apply calibration question. If weak, revise or discard.

**R4 — Confidence Discipline**
Low confidence limits how category may be used. Favor boundary-setting and failure explanation.

## Categories

### A — Behavioral Economics (Decision-Making Under Cognitive Constraint)
**Gerd Gigerenzer**: Ecological rationality — when heuristics outperform optimization.
Calibration: "Did I specify when this heuristic works and when it fails?"

**Daniel Kahneman**: Systematic judgment errors under uncertainty.
Calibration: "Did I name a bias without explaining how it distorts payoffs or decisions?"

### B — Microeconomics (Incentives, Allocation, Trade-Offs)
**Michael Mauboussin**: Separating skill from luck; expectations vs outcomes.
Calibration: "Am I confusing a good outcome with a good decision?"

**Mancur Olson**: Collective action failure and incentive incompatibility.
Calibration: "Why would a rational actor not support this, even if it's collectively optimal?"

### C — Game Theory & Strategic Interaction
**Thomas Schelling**: Commitment, coordination, credibility, expectation management.
Calibration: "How does this decision change what others believe I will do?"

### D — Macroeconomics (Aggregates, Cycles, System-Level Constraints)
**Howard Marks**: Cyclicality, irreversibility, downside risk.
Calibration: "What breaks first if this system-level assumption is wrong?"

### E — Institutional Economics
**Douglass North**: Institutional persistence, path dependence, incentive lock-in.
Calibration: "What institutional constraint makes this behavior rational to maintain?"

### F — Development Economics
**Esther Duflo**: Why interventions fail when generalized; dominance of local constraints.
Calibration: "Which local constraint breaks this intervention when generalized?"

### G — Decision-Making Under Uncertainty
**Howard Marks**: Asymmetric risk, irreversibility, second-order effects.
Calibration: "Where is the downside unbounded?"

### H — Strategy & Internal Memo Density
**Ben Thompson**: Constraint-driven strategy with memo-level density.
Calibration: "Does this read like a working note meant to clarify a real decision?"

### I — M&A (Low Confidence — Constraint-Only Use)
**Bruce Greenwald**: Asset value vs strategic value; real vs imagined synergies.
Calibration: "What value exists only because of ownership change?"

### J — Stock Market (Boundary Category)
**Benjamin Graham**: Distinguishing price movements from information.
Calibration: "Is this price movement informative or merely expressive?"

### K — Finance (Agency & Capital Allocation)
**Michael Jensen**: Agency costs and internal incentive misalignment.
Calibration: "Who controls capital, and how are they rewarded for deploying it?"

### PG — Paul Graham
**Analytical Function**: Finding the "naughty" or counter-intuitive truth everyone knows but ignores.
Calibration: "Is this idea surprising to the writer?"

---

## Operational Use

1. Select one primary category per piece
2. Apply its calibration question explicitly
3. Treat low-confidence categories as claim limiters, not amplifiers
4. If a piece resists calibration, it is under-specified
