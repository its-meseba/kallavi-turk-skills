Evidence Bank v1.0 (Decision Contexts, Constraints, and Confidence Boundaries)

Purpose
This document defines the empirical boundary of analysis. It is not a résumé, a memory log, or a source of anecdotes. It exists to answer one question only: In what kinds of decision environments do I actually know what breaks, and why?

Structural Rules (Non-Negotiable)
- No chronology
- No outcomes
- No achievements
- No narrative framing
- No personal storytelling

Everything is expressed as context → constraint → failure exposure.

---

Layer 1 — Decision Contexts

C1 — Capital Allocation Under Constraint
Decisions involving finite capital, competing uses of funds, irreversible commitments.
Typical tensions: runway vs optionality, investment vs preservation, precision vs speed.

C2 — Strategy Formation Under Uncertainty
Decisions where objectives are clear but paths are not, feedback is delayed/noisy.
Typical tensions: commitment vs flexibility, signaling vs execution, coherence vs adaptability.

C3 — Market Entry, Positioning, and Segmentation
Decisions involving unclear demand boundaries, heterogeneous preferences, incomplete info.
Typical tensions: focus vs reach, depth vs breadth, differentiation vs comparability.

C4 — Pricing and Value Communication
Decisions where willingness to pay is uncertain, price signals and filters.
Typical tensions: learning vs monetization, accessibility vs seriousness, volume vs margin.

C5 — Organizational and Incentive Design
Decisions involving role definition, responsibility allocation, incentive alignment.
Typical tensions: autonomy vs control, alignment vs accountability, speed vs coordination.

C6 — Institutional and Rule-Bound Environments
Decisions constrained by formal rules, informal norms, enforcement mechanisms.
Typical tensions: feasibility vs desirability, reform vs stability, compliance vs circumvention.

C7 — Early-Stage Company Decisions
Decisions involving extreme information scarcity, overlapping roles, compressed time horizons.
Typical tensions: experimentation vs coherence, speed vs reversibility, conviction vs evidence.

---

Layer 2 — Observed Constraints

K1 — Capital Scarcity: Capital limits option value, error tolerance, learning cycles.
K2 — Information Asymmetry: Info is unevenly distributed, costly to acquire, often misinterpreted.
K3 — Incentive Misalignment: Actors respond to what they're rewarded/protected from.
K4 — Time Pressure: Time compresses deliberation, biases action, penalizes reversibility.
K5 — Cognitive Load: Limited attention, competing priorities, emotional stress affect decisions.
K6 — Institutional Inertia: Rules persist beyond usefulness, reward compliance over optimization.

---

Layer 3 — Recurrent Failure Modes

F1 — Treating Feedback as Demand: Positive signals misread as willingness to pay/commitment.
F2 — Over-Optimization Before Problem Definition: Resources spent refining before problem is specified.
F3 — Confusing Alignment with Enforcement: Shared goals assumed to substitute for contracts/incentives.
F4 — Delaying Irreversible Decisions Excessively: Fear leads to option value decay, strategic drift.
F5 — Outcome-Based Retrospective Justification: Decisions judged by results/timing/luck not ex-ante quality.

---

Layer 4 — Confidence Boundaries

B1 — Domains Suitable for Structural Analysis Only: framing, constraint identification, failure explanation. No prescriptions/optimization/forecasts. Includes: public equity markets, deal structuring, complex financial instruments.

B2 — Domains Where Case-Specific Claims Are Avoided: mechanism-level, incentive-based, non-technical. Avoid: numerical specificity, model outputs, performance claims.

B3 — Domains Where Silence Is Preferred: topics requiring authority, deep specialization, or insider info.

Meta-Rule: If a claim cannot be traced to at least one decision context and one constraint, it should not be made publicly.
